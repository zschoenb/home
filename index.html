Zachary Schoenberger

I am a Metadata Analyst at University of Alberta Libraries. This role involves creating, manipulating, transforming, storing, and transmitting metadata for the university's digital assets. My work is particularly aimed at supporting the university's Hydra-based institutional repository.

I get excited by all things data, especially data visualization, linked data, network analysis, and using scripting languages like R and Python to solve problems (or, to create new ones!).

I am interested in the intersection between information services and the digital humanities. I think a lot about how librarians and digital humanities can work together, how we might learn from our respective practices, and I wonder, where are we heading? This interest is informed by my MLIS and MA in Digital Humanities, my ongoing research activites, and my practical experience as a Metadata Analyst.

In my spare time, I love to travel (who doesn't?), to read, and to play a video game every now and then.
